Technology, Mining & Waste

With Gerry McGovern

Tuesday, June 2 — 12:00 PM (Eastern Time)

Modern technology is often presented as clean, efficient, and essential to solving environmental problems. But what are the hidden material costs behind batteries, copper mining, aluminum, AI systems, data centers, and electronic waste?

Join Hart Hagan in conversation with author Gerry McGovern for a calm and factual discussion of how modern technology affects land, water, forests, energy, and future generations.

Topics Include

  • Mining and the growing demand for copper and metals
  • The environmental footprint of AI and data centers
  • Electronic waste and toxic disposal systems
  • The limits of “green growth”
  • How wiser choices may still be possible

About Gerry McGovern

Gerry McGovern is the author of 99th Day: A Warning About Technology and World Wide Waste. He is internationally known for helping organizations reduce waste and improve usefulness through his Top Tasks method.
